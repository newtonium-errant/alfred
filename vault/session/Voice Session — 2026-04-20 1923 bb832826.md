**Alfred** (19:42): Appended to `note/Exercise Plan Started 2026-04-20.md`.

On your TGU question — yes, shoulder packing is exactly the cue. Before you press off the floor, you want the bell-side shoulder actively pulled down and back into the socket (scapular depression + retraction), like you're trying to put your shoulder blade in your back pocket. Arm stays vertical, elbow locked, wrist neutral. The instability you felt is almost always that shoulder floating up toward the ear instead of being anchored. Pavel and StrongFirst coaches call it "putting the shoulder in your pocket." Worth watching a Hardstyle TGU breakdown on that specifically before your next session.
