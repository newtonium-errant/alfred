### Key Insights
- Medicine is fundamentally rubric-based — most diagnoses, documentation, and communication follow pre-defined criteria, which makes it highly amenable to AI agent automation
- The current AI scribe stops at transcription and terminology formatting but does not analyze for clinical gaps or suggest next steps — that gap is the core unmet need
- A truly useful medical AI agent would need to do three things simultaneously: transcribe, suggest missing clinical elements based on medical knowledge, and proactively prepare downstream tasks (referrals, lab orders) before the encounter ends
